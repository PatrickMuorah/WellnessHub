Regular Brisk Walks and Other Exercise Can Help Prevent Falls in Older Women

New study finds that being active 30 minutes a day, 5 days a week can help prevent falls and injuries in older women.

Older women who exercised the equivalent of 30 minutes a day, five times a week were up to one-third less likely to hurt themselves in a fall, according to an Australian study published today in JAMA Network Open. Best Exercises for Senior Citizens

Exercises to help senior citizens

Water Aerobics: Exercising in water is a great way to move your body if you’re worried about arthritis and joint pain. The water helps support your body weight, and it also provides a natural resistance, eliminating the need to use additional weights. With water aerobics, you can improve your balance, flexibility, and strength. Some exercises to consider in water aerobics are leg lifts, arm circles, and walking in place. Chair Yoga Yoga has been scientifically proven to not only have therapeutic effects but to also improve the quality of life of those who practice it. Given inaccurate assumptions about yoga, however, many people think that they are not capable of doing yoga, especially older adults.
